In this class, you will be given an article each Monday* that you will work with throughout the week during the first 5-7 minutes of class each day following the daily instructions below:

Monday – Read the article and summarize the moral dilemma being faced, including the two sides to the given issue.

Tuesday – Compose three to five questions the people in the article should ask themselves, in their effort to make a good moral decision. (Be specific and concrete in these posed questions, not vague.)

Wednesday – Answer those three to five questions (from the perspective of that person in the article), and be sure to include possible consequences of any decision that could be made on both sides of the moral issue.

Thursday – How do you think Jesus/the Church would respond to this moral issue? Again, be specific and concrete, even to the point of citing Scripture, if you are able.

Friday – What would you do in this situation?  Again, be specific, and give reasons to back up your decision.  

The above written work will be done each day during the first 5-7 minutes of class. When you arrive each day for class, during those first 5-7 minutes, it is expected that you work on these questions. If you do not get done in the time allotted, you are to finish it for homework. If you are absent on a given day, you are to make up that work, since you know in advance what is expected of you each day. Make certain to separate your daily entries so that I can easily see what day you are working on.

If you are absent for an extended period of time, you will be expected to make up all the work on these articles.

This work will be due at the beginning of class on Friday of each week. Therefore, you must have it completed within the first 5 minutes of Friday’s class. If time permits, we will then use part of class on Fridays to discuss the articles.
